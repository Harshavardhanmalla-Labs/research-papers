Paper~5 reported that EPSS-only vulnerability prioritization decays
from mean Precision@50 of $0.331$ to $0.034$ over six maintenance
windows at a single capacity-arrival operating point ($K = 50$,
$\lambda = 3$). This paper sweeps a pre-registered $5 \times 4$ grid,
$K \in \{10, 25, 50, 100, 200\}$ and $\lambda \in \{1, 3, 6, 12\}$,
on 25 evaluation seeds, producing 15{,}000 result rows. EPSS-only
exhibits a negative decay slope in all 20 cells (mean slope range
$-0.025$ to $-0.052$ P@50/window), and the predicted monotone scaling
of decay with $K$ (H1) and $\lambda$ (H2) is rejected. Most
importantly, the pre-registered claim that HygienePrio-full retains
W6 P@50 $\geq 0.40$ across all cells (H4) is rejected: at
$K \leq 50$ HygienePrio-full holds a W6 floor of $\geq 0.473$, but at
$K \in \{100, 200\}$ it collapses alongside EPSS-only (to $0.062$ at
$K = 200$, $\lambda = 1$), degrading at high capacity in the same way
exploit-likelihood scoring does \emph{under self-driven (closed-loop)
selection}. Per-pair, HygienePrio-full still outperforms EPSS-only in
$96.0\%$ of (cell, seed, window) triples on the synthetic fixture; a
real-distribution capacity sweep (CISA KEV and FIRST.org EPSS
attributes, 6{,}750 rows) shows this persistence is a
synthetic-distribution artifact, falling to $10.0\%$ with the two
methods tied within $0.5$~pp in $69\%$ of triples. All claims remain
bounded to the synthetic evaluation context; external validation on
real fleet telemetry with longitudinal exploitation outcomes is
required before any deployment recommendation.
