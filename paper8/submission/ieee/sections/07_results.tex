The summary statistic across $w \geq 2$ per cell:

\midrule
50  & $+1.04$ & $+0.53$ \\
100 & $+0.99$ & $-1.37$ \\
200 & $-0.66$ & $-0.94$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

EWMA-3 underperforms \textsf{lag1} at every cell. At $K = 50$ the
gap is $-0.51$ in recovery-ratio units; at $K = 100$ the gap is
$-2.36$, the largest single-cell delta in the table; at $K = 200$ the
gap is $-0.28$. The naive prediction ``smoothing helps at high
capacity, hurts mildly at low capacity'' is contradicted in both directions.

\subsection{Per-Window Detail}

\textbf{$K = 50$.} EWMA-3 matches \textsf{lag1} at W2 and W3 but
falls below it at W4--W6 by $0.6$--$2.6$~pp. The mechanism is that
the W4 calibration target for EWMA-3 includes W1, W2, and W3 of the
calibration seeds, whose distributions have shifted by W4. The
\textsf{lag1} W4 calibration target uses only the W3 calibration
state, which is closer to the W4 evaluation state.

\textbf{$K = 100$.} The gap widens. At W3 \textsf{lag1} adds
$+3.0$~pp over \textsf{fixed}; EWMA-3 \textit{loses} $-0.3$~pp.
By W6, \textsf{lag1} gains $+4.0$~pp while EWMA-3 holds essentially
zero ($+0.0$~pp). The H2 cell ($K = 100, w = 5$) is also where
\textsf{ewma3} and \textsf{trail3} diverge by $+10.6$~pp ---
the only window in the entire sweep where the smoothing-strategy
choice is first-order.

\textbf{$K = 200$.} Both \textsf{lag1} and EWMA-3 are hazardous,
but EWMA-3 worse: at W2, \textsf{lag1} loses $-5.9$~pp, EWMA-3
loses $-6.0$~pp; at W3, \textsf{lag1} loses $-7.8$~pp, EWMA-3 loses
$-8.0$~pp. The point estimates are very close, but the average over
$w \geq 2$ tilts further negative for EWMA-3 because the W4--W6
recovery present in \textsf{lag1} (positive deltas) is dampened in
EWMA-3.

\subsection{Hypothesis Outcomes}

\input{tables/tab_hypotheses}

All four pre-registered hypotheses are rejected.

\textbf{H1 (EWMA-3 $\geq$ fixed at $K = 200$).} Rejected. Two
window failures at $w \in \{2, 3\}$ with deltas of $-6.0$ and
$-8.0$~pp.

\textbf{H2 (EWMA-3 matches \textsf{lag1} at moderate capacity).}
Rejected. Five cell-window failures across $K \in \{50, 100\}$ with
$|\Delta| > 0.02$. The signs are negative (EWMA-3 worse than
\textsf{lag1}) in all five.

\textbf{H3 (EWMA-3 matches \textsf{trail3}).} Rejected at one
cell-window ($K = 100, w = 5$). The single failure is large
($+10.6$~pp) and reflects a specific grid-search disagreement at
that window; otherwise the smoothing-strategy choice between EWMA
and trail3 is consistently second-order.

\textbf{H4 (EWMA-3 recovery $\geq 0.5$ at $K = 200$).} Rejected.
Cell-mean recovery ratio is $-0.94$, negative rather than $\geq 0.5$.

\subsection{Smoothing-Penalty Mechanism}

The per-cell pattern is monotone in history length:
\textsf{lag1} $>$ \textsf{trail3} $\approx$ \textsf{ewma3} at every
$K$. The pre-registered EWMA $\alpha = 0.6$ effectively averages
$\approx 2.5$ past windows; the equal-weight \textsf{trail3} averages
exactly 3. Both bring older calibration-seed states into the
fitting target, and at the simulated bi-weekly cadence those older
states are systematically less representative of the current window
than the most recent past window alone. The H3 single failure at
$K = 100, w = 5$ is the exception that confirms the rule: at that
window the grid-search optimum was on a flat region and the
$\alpha = 0.6$ weighting moved EWMA's selection to a different local
optimum than trail3's equal weighting.
