\subsection{H1 Supported: Feasibility Region Reached}

The three pre-registered tolerances are simultaneously satisfied:
\begin{itemize}
\item Worst per-window K=200 $\Delta_{\mathrm{cap-fix}} = 0.0$
(tolerance $\geq -0.01$).
\item Cell-mean K=50 $\Delta_{\mathrm{cap-lag1}} = 0.0$ exactly
(tolerance $\geq -0.02$).
\item Cell-mean K=100 $\Delta_{\mathrm{cap-lag1}} = -0.004$
(tolerance $\geq -0.02$).
\end{itemize}

The K=50 result holds with equality because $\tau_{50} = 0.20$
suppresses every fire at K=50; the detector never reverts to fixed,
so cap\_aware uses lag-1 weights at every window and reproduces the
lag-1 strategy exactly. The cell-mean delta is therefore $0.0$ by
construction.

This is the first procedure in the Papers~7--12 sequence to reach
the joint feasibility region.

\subsection{H3 Supported: Perfectly Monotone Firing}

Fire fractions are $\{0\%, 40\%, 100\%\}$ at $K \in \{50, 100, 200\}$,
yielding Spearman $\rho = 1.0$ ($p = 0$, exact monotonicity). The
$\tau_K$ vector operationalises capacity-conditional behaviour
exactly: at K=50 the threshold $\tau_{50} = 0.20$ is large enough
that the calibration-target shift $|\Delta_w|$ never crosses it; at
K=200 the threshold $\tau_{200} = 0.02$ is small enough that
$|\Delta_w|$ always crosses it; at K=100 the intermediate
$\tau_{100} = 0.05$ produces a 40\% firing rate.

\subsection{H4 Rejected: cap\_aware Equals gated at K=200}

The cap\_aware K=200 cell-mean P@50 is $0.2664$; the gated K=200
cell-mean is also $0.2664$, identical to four decimal places. The
H4 pre-registered tolerance required a margin of $+0.01$;
the observed margin is $0.000$.

The mechanism is structural. At $\tau_{200} = 0.02$ the magnitude
test fires at every post-W1 K=200 window because the per-window
$|\Delta_w|$ values are $\{0.144, 0.160, 0.020, 0.096, 0.136\}$ at
$w = 2, \ldots, 6$, all $\geq 0.02$. cap\_aware therefore reverts to
fixed at every K=200 window --- exactly what the static gate does
unconditionally at K=200. The two strategies execute the same decision
on every window-seed pair.

\subsection{H2 Rejected at One Cell-Window}

At $K=200, w=4$ the calibration-target shift is $|\Delta| = 0.020$.
The cap\_aware threshold $\tau_{200} = 0.02$ fires; the adaptive05
threshold $\tau = 0.05$ does not. cap\_aware therefore reverts to
fixed at this window; adaptive05 uses the lag-1 fit. The lag-1 fit
at $w = 4, K = 200$ happens to be beneficial: adaptive05 records
$0.288$ vs cap\_aware's $0.254$ ($-3.4$~pp).

This single window failure is intrinsic to the pre-registered
$\tau_{200} = 0.02$ choice: any threshold value below $0.05$ that
maintains the H1 feasibility would have produced the same fire
decision at this window. The K=200 cell-mean across all six windows
remains close to adaptive05 ($0.2664$ vs $0.2733$), but the
$-0.034$ pre-registered tolerance was missed.

\subsection{Substantive Interpretation}

The H1 positive and H4 rejection together describe the practical
contribution: \textbf{the capacity-aware detector reaches the
feasibility region precisely by approximating the static gate at the
two capacity extremes}. At K=50 cap\_aware $=$ lag1 (no fire); at
K=200 cap\_aware $=$ fixed (all fire). The only substantive
per-window decision is at K=100, where cap\_aware loses $-0.4$~pp
to lag-1 on average due to its 40\% intervention rate.

A practitioner deploying the static gate (lag-1 at $K \leq 100$,
fixed at $K \geq 200$) accepts the cap\_aware $-0.4$~pp K=100 cost
implicitly --- and avoids the H2 single-window failure at K=200.
The static gate is therefore not just feasible but slightly preferred
in this grid; cap\_aware validates the gate's feasibility without
improving on its operating point.
