\subsection{H2 Substantively Positive: CUSUM Beats the Static Gate}

The K=200 cell-mean P@50 of \textsf{cusum} is $0.2754$; the K=200
cell-mean P@50 of \textsf{gated} is $0.2664$. The margin is
$+0.0090$~pp.

This is the first detector in the Papers~7--13 sequence that beats
the static gate at K=200. The pre-registered H2 binary test required
$\geq +0.01$ pp; the observed margin falls $0.001$ pp short and the
formal test is rejected. We do not relax the threshold; we report
the substantive direction (CUSUM is strictly better than gated by
an operationally meaningful margin) as the substantive contribution.

\subsection{H3 Supported: Selective Firing at K=200}

CUSUM fires at K=200 in $60\%$ of post-W1 windows; cap\_aware fires
in $100\%$. H3 (strict inequality) is supported.

The per-window decisions trace as predicted: at K=200 the cp\_delta
sequence is $\{0.144, 0.160, 0.020, 0.096, 0.136\}$ at $w = 2, ..., 6$.
CUSUM accumulator updates with slack $k = 0.04$:
\begin{itemize}
\item W2: $C_2 = 0.104 \geq 0.10$, fire, reset.
\item W3: $C_3 = 0.120 \geq 0.10$, fire, reset.
\item W4: $C_4 = \max(0, 0 + 0.020 - 0.040) = 0$, no fire. \textbf{W4
is the noise-excursion window where CUSUM captures lag-1 benefit
that cap\_aware misses.}
\item W5: $C_5 = 0 + 0.096 - 0.040 = 0.056 < 0.10$, no fire.
\item W6: $C_6 = 0.056 + 0.136 - 0.040 = 0.152 \geq 0.10$, fire.
\end{itemize}

CUSUM fires at W2, W3, W6 (3 of 5) and refrains at W4, W5 (2 of 5).
At W4 and W5 CUSUM uses lag-1 weights; at K=200 those lag-1 fits
contribute the $+0.9$~pp gain over the static gate.

\subsection{H4 Supported: CUSUM Strictly Above cap\_aware at K=200}

CUSUM K=200 cell mean ($0.2754$) strictly exceeds cap\_aware's
($0.2664$). The margin is $+0.9$~pp, comfortably above the $-0.01$
within-noise tolerance.

\subsection{H1 Rejected: K=50 Over-Firing}

CUSUM fires at K=50 in $20\%$ of post-W1 windows (compared to
cap\_aware's $0\%$, which uses the larger $\tau_{50} = 0.20$). At
K=50 the cell-mean cusum--lag1 is $-3.9$~pp, missing the $-2.0$~pp
feasibility tolerance.

The mechanism is straightforward. At K=50, $|\Delta_w|$ values are
similar in magnitude to K=200 in some windows (both regimes
experience large early-window calibration-target shifts at W2),
but at K=50 lag-1 recalibration is beneficial whereas at K=200 it
harms. A single $(k, h)$ pair that fires usefully at K=200 also
fires harmfully at K=50.

The K=50 cell-mean is $-3.9$~pp below lag-1 because in the windows
where CUSUM fires, it reverts to fixed and loses the lag-1 gain. The
$-3.9$~pp is essentially the K=50 lag-1 advantage from Paper~7
applied with $20\%$ weight by the firing rate.

\subsection{Substantive Interpretation}

CUSUM is the first detector in the Papers~7--13 sequence that
simultaneously (a) beats the static gate at K=200 by an operationally
meaningful margin and (b) breaks the structural-collapse property
that the magnitude detector exhibits at small $\tau_{200}$. The
trade-off is K=50 over-firing.

The H1 K=50 failure and H2 fine-margin rejection point to the same
fix: a per-K CUSUM $(k_K, h_K)$ vector. A larger $k_{50}$ would
suppress K=50 firing without affecting K=200; a larger $h_{50}$
would have the same effect. The capacity-aware CUSUM family is the
natural Paper~14 candidate.

\subsection{Pre-Registration Deviations}

None. CUSUM parameters $(k, h)$, strategy set, hypotheses, decision
rules, and stop rules were applied verbatim. The H2 fine-margin
rejection and H1 K=50-failure rejection were reported as observed
without threshold relaxation.
