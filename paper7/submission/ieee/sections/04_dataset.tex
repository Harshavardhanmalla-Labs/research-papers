\section{Dataset and Sweep Grid}

\subsection{Synthetic Fleet}

All experiments use the EEHDA synthetic fleet generator from
Paper~4~\cite{paper4hygieneprio}: 1{,}000 users, 830 hosts, $\sim
3{,}500$ (host, CVE) pairs at Window~1, DBIR-calibrated patch lags,
NVD-calibrated CVSS distribution, $\sim$12\% of CVEs with
EPSS~$> 0.10$, $\sim$8\% KEV prevalence. New CVE disclosures at
$w > 1$ are drawn from the same fixture distributions.

\subsection{Seed Split}

The split is identical to Paper~5~\cite{paper5temporal} and is
shared across all six experiments: 5 calibration seeds (100--104)
for weight-fitting only, 25 evaluation seeds (105--129) for
reporting. No evaluation seed is ever used for fitting.

\subsection{Selection Policy}

In every experiment, HygienePrio-full under \textit{fixed} Paper~4
weights drives the top-$K$ selection at every window. The
calibration strategy under evaluation affects the scoring weights
only, not the fleet evolution. All strategies in any experiment see
the same evolving backlog, isolating the recalibration decision.
Selection-policy coupling is restated as an internal-validity threat
in \S\ref{sec:threats}; the cross-method trajectory question is
addressed separately by Paper~9~\cite{paper9selftraj}.
