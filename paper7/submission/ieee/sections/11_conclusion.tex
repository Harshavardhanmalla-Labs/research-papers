\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A six-experiment pre-registered evaluation of online recalibration
for HygienePrio produces 22{,}500 frozen result rows, 27 locked
hypotheses, and a single sharpened deployable recipe. The
contribution is the cumulative arc, not any single experiment.

\textbf{What the program closes.}
E1 establishes lag-1 as a deployable substitute that recovers the
Paper~5 offline-peek ceiling at $K \leq 100$ ($\rho \approx 1.0$)
but harms performance at $K = 200$ ($\rho = -0.66$). E2 falsifies
the smoothing prior --- EWMA-3 and trail-3 amplify the K=200 hazard
rather than reversing it. E3 introduces the magnitude detector,
avoids the K=200 hazard at a K=50 cost, and is the first procedure
to beat the static gate at K=200 by $+0.7$~pp. E4 proves no single
$\tau$ can reach the joint feasibility region. E5 reaches feasibility
with a per-K $\tau_K$ vector but collapses to the static gate.
E6 introduces CUSUM, breaks the static-gate collapse at K=200 by
$+0.9$~pp, and exposes the K=50 over-firing as the next failure
mode.

\textbf{The deployable recipe.}
At $K \leq 100$ deploy lag-1 online recalibration directly.
At $K = 200$ deploy CUSUM($k = 0.04, h = 0.10$) for $+0.9$~pp
over the static gate. For mixed-capacity deployments a per-K
$(k_K, h_K)$ CUSUM vector under pre-registration is the natural
successor. The recipe is capacity-conditional and bounded to the
synthetic evaluation context; external validation on real fleet
telemetry with longitudinal exploitation outcome data is required
before any production deployment.

\textbf{Reproducibility.} All six experiments are independently
reproducible from their pre-registered seed lists. The
\texttt{paper7/experiments/0[1-6]\_*/} directories ship the
per-experiment protocol, runner, frozen results CSV, analysis
script, and figures. The consolidated artifact is available at
\url{https://github.com/Harshavardhanmalla-Labs/research-papers/tree/main/paper7}.

\textbf{Future work.} (i) Per-K CUSUM $(k_K, h_K)$ vector under
pre-registration --- direct H1.E6 fix. (ii) Two-sided CUSUM,
EWMA-CUSUM, Bayesian online change-point detectors. (iii)
Sensitivity sweeps over the CUSUM $(k, h)$ landscape and the EWMA
$\alpha$ axis. (iv) Closed-loop calibration where the selection
policy is also recalibrated, addressing the selection-policy
coupling threat that all six experiments inherit. (v) Real fleet
validation with longitudinal exploitation outcome data.
