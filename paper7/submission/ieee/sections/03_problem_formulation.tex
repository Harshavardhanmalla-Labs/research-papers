\section{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:problem}

\subsection{Setting}

We inherit Paper~5's~\cite{paper5temporal} multi-window simulation
($W = 6$ bi-weekly windows on the EEHDA fleet) and Paper~6's~\cite{paper6capacity}
$(K, \lambda)$ parameterisation. The cell-defining parameters are
fixed at $\lambda = 3$ Poisson new-CVE arrivals per window, and
$K \in \{50, 100, 200\}$. The HygienePrio scorer Eq.~(\ref{eq:hp}) and
HRS components are inherited verbatim from Paper~4~\cite{paper4hygieneprio};
the only quantity that varies across strategies in this paper is the
scorer weights $(\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta)$.

\subsection{Three Calibration Strategies}

For each cell and each window $w$, we evaluate three strategies:

\begin{description}
\item[\textsf{fixed}] Weights remain at the Paper~4 calibrated values
$(\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta) = (0.7, 0.5, 0.1, 0.2)$ at every
window. No refitting.
\item[\textsf{online}] At $w = 1$, weights are \textsf{fixed}.
At $w \geq 2$, grid-search the scorer weights to maximise mean P@50 on
the five calibration seeds at \textit{window~$w-1$ state} --- the
strictly past observation. Apply the fitted weights to the 25
evaluation seeds at \textit{window~$w$}.
\item[\textsf{offline}] At $w = 1$, weights are \textsf{fixed}.
At $w \geq 2$, grid-search at the calibration seeds' \textit{window~$w$
state} (peek) and apply to the evaluation seeds at the same window. This
replicates Paper~5's H3 procedure and acts as an upper-bound ceiling.
\end{description}

The grid is identical to Paper~5~\cite{paper5temporal} (108 points across
$\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta$).

\subsection{Ground Truth and Metric}

Ground truth, per-window HRS-threshold recomputation, and Precision@50
metric are identical to Papers~5 and~6 and are not restated here.

\subsection{Pre-Registered Hypotheses}

\begin{description}
\item[H1] Online $\geq$ fixed at every (cell, window) pair --- online
should never be worse than fixed by more than $1$~pp.
\item[H2] Online $\leq$ offline at every (cell, window) pair --- online
should not exceed the offline-peek ceiling by more than $1$~pp.
\item[H3] At high-capacity cells ($K \in \{100, 200\}$), online
recovers at least 50\% of the (offline $-$ fixed) gap for at least one
window $w \in \{4, 5, 6\}$, with absolute gain $\geq 5$~pp.
\end{description}

Decision rules and stop rules are in \texttt{paper7/design/PAPER7\_PROTOCOL.md}.
