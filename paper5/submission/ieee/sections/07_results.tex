\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Temporal P@50 Trajectories}

Table~\ref{tab:pk50_window} and Fig.~\ref{fig:trajectory} report mean P@50 per method across the six maintenance windows over 25 evaluation seeds, computed from the frozen results CSV (\texttt{paper5/results/primary\_full\_v1/temporal\_results.csv}). Four robust patterns emerge.

\textit{1) EPSS decay.} EPSS-only decays monotonically from $0.331$ at Window~1 to $0.034$ at Window~6 --- an 89.6\% relative drop. Once the highest-EPSS CVEs are remediated from the backlog, the remaining CVEs have systematically lower EPSS scores and the binary ground-truth condition (EPSS~$> 0.10$) is satisfied by an increasingly small fraction of the residual backlog, so EPSS-only ranking becomes uninformative.

\textit{2) HygienePrio resilience.} HygienePrio-full maintains mean P@50 $\geq 0.499$ at every window (W1--W6: $0.595, 0.544, 0.564, 0.514, 0.521, 0.499$). The trajectory is not strictly monotone --- W3 ($0.564$) slightly exceeds W2 ($0.544$), and W5 ($0.521$) slightly exceeds W4 ($0.514$) --- reflecting the interaction between new-CVE arrivals and the per-window recomputed HRS-percentile ground-truth threshold; the variation is well within the per-seed bootstrap CI band.

\textit{3) Growing absolute gap.} The HygienePrio-vs-EPSS gap is $26.4$ pp at Window~1 and grows to $46.5$ pp at Window~6. The gap widens because HygienePrio's own P@50 stays near $0.5$ while EPSS-only's collapses; the result is that the operational advantage of HygienePrio increases with each additional patching cycle.

\textit{4) HRS-only overtakes EPSS at Window~2.} HRS-only is $0.284$ at Window~1 (below EPSS-only's $0.331$) but $0.229$ at Window~2 (above EPSS-only's $0.149$), and remains above EPSS-only for every subsequent window. For the entire second-through-sixth window range, the simplest hygiene baseline outperforms exploit-likelihood scoring under the evaluated capacity.

\subsection{Advantage Persistence}

HygienePrio-full outperforms EPSS-only at P@50 in \textbf{150 of 150 window-seed pairs} (25 seeds $\times$ 6 windows). The per-seed dominance is therefore not eroded by temporal evolution.

\subsection{Stability Analysis}

Fig.~\ref{fig:stability} reports the per-seed standard deviation of P@50 across the six windows for each method. HygienePrio-full's per-seed standard deviations are concentrated and substantially lower than EPSS-only's (mean per-seed sd $0.075$ versus $0.118$); EPSS-only's larger per-seed sd reflects the wide span of its trajectory, from relatively high P@50 at Window~1 to near-zero by Windows~5--6. The stability advantage matters operationally: a remediation team relying on HygienePrio-full faces predictable scheduling quality across the patching cycle, whereas an EPSS-only team faces effectively-random ranking by the fourth window.

\subsection{EPSS Decay Mechanism}

The decay of EPSS-only reflects a deterministic compositional effect, not stochastic noise. HygienePrio-full's policy selects the highest-EPSS-on-high-HRS-host pairs first; remediating those pairs preferentially removes the high-EPSS tail from the backlog. By Window~3, the fraction of unpatched pairs satisfying $\mathrm{EPSS} > 0.10$ has dropped substantially, and the per-window ground truth (which retains this threshold) now identifies a smaller positive set drawn from a low-EPSS bulk. EPSS-only ranking on the residual bulk is near-random with respect to that smaller positive set. The mechanism is robust to bootstrap resampling.

\subsection{Hypothesis Outcomes}

\textbf{H1 (sustained advantage).} Supported: HygienePrio-full's CI does not overlap EPSS-only's in 6 of 6 windows, exceeding the pre-registered 4-of-6 threshold.

\textbf{H2 (monotonic attenuation).} \textit{Rejected (direction reversed).} H2 predicted that the HygienePrio-vs-EPSS advantage would be largest at Window~1 and attenuate monotonically; the observed advantage is instead \textit{smallest} at Window~1 ($26.4$ pp) and grows to $46.5$ pp at Window~6. HygienePrio's own P@50 declines from $0.595$ to $0.499$ approximately monotonically, with two minor reversals (W3 and W5), but the hypothesised attenuation of the advantage did not occur. The prediction failed in the favorable direction: the advantage strengthens rather than erodes over the patching cycle.

\textbf{H3 (recalibration not needed).} \textit{Rejected.} The per-window recalibration ablation (Table~\ref{tab:recalibration}) shows that grid-searching scorer weights on the five calibration seeds at each window's evolved state and applying those weights to the 25 evaluation seeds improves mean P@50 by $+17.4$ pp at W1, $+21.3$ pp at W2, $+10.1$ pp at W3, and $+9.0$ pp at W4. These deltas all exceed the pre-registered $5$ pp stop-rule threshold, so the hypothesis that the initial calibration is sufficiently stable is rejected. The gains shrink to $+2.9$ pp by W5 and $+1.3$ pp by W6, where the residual backlog has thinned and per-window calibration has fewer degrees of freedom to exploit. The $+17.4$ pp delta at W1 --- before any temporal evolution has occurred --- additionally indicates that the inherited Paper~4 weights were miscalibrated for this fixture from the outset (consistent with the fixture differences noted in \S6.3), so the recalibration gains are not a purely temporal effect.
