\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Scorer (Inherited)}

The HygienePrio scorer~\cite{paper4hygieneprio} is inherited
verbatim:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
S(h,c) =\;& \alpha\,\mathrm{EPSS}(c) + \beta\,\mathrm{HRS}(h) \\
          & + \gamma\,\mathrm{KEV\_recency}(c) \\
          & + \delta\,\bigl(\mathrm{EPSS}(c) \cdot \mathrm{HRS}(h)\bigr).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:hp}
\end{equation}
HRS combines patch posture (0.50), AD exposure (0.30), telemetry
freshness (0.20). Only the four scorer weights are subject to
recalibration.

\subsection{Change-Point Detector}

At window $w \geq 2$, the detector executes:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Fit candidate lag-1 weights by grid search to maximise mean
P@50 on the five calibration seeds at window $w - 1$. Record the
fitted weights $g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w$ and the maximum mean
$p^{\text{cal}}_{w-1}(g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w)$.
\item Compute a backward reference $p^{\text{ref}}_w$:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item If $w = 2$: $p^{\text{ref}}_w$ = mean P@50 of the
  \textit{fixed} Paper~4 weights at calibration seeds' window
  $w - 1$.
  \item Otherwise: $p^{\text{ref}}_w$ = mean P@50 of $g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w$
  applied to calibration seeds' window $w - 2$.
  \end{itemize}
\item Compute $\Delta_w = p^{\text{cal}}_{w-1}(g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w)
- p^{\text{ref}}_w$.
\item \textbf{Fire} the change-point if $|\Delta_w| \geq \tau$
($\tau = 0.05$, pre-registered).
\end{enumerate}

The intuition is that $|\Delta_w|$ measures the rate at which the
lag-1-fit weights' calibration-target P@50 has shifted between two
consecutive past windows. A large shift indicates that the
distribution at $w - 1$ is meaningfully different from the
distribution at $w - 2$, so the lag-1 fit is likely to be poorly
generalised to $w$ as well.

\subsection{Strategy Decision Rule}

At each window $w$:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textsf{adaptive} weights = $g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w$ if the
detector did not fire, else Paper~4 \textsf{fixed} weights.
\item \textsf{gated} weights = $g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w$ if $K \leq 100$,
else Paper~4 \textsf{fixed} weights. The gated decision is
independent of the detector and of the window.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Fleet-State Evolution}

Identical to Papers~7 and~8: HygienePrio-full under \textsf{fixed}
weights drives the fleet top-$K$ selection at every window for every
strategy, so all five strategies score against the same evolving
backlog. Selection-policy coupling is restated as a threat in
\S\ref{sec:threats}.

\subsection{Logged Quantities}

Per (cell, window) the run logs: $\Delta_w$, the fire decision, the
fitted $g^{\textsf{lag1}}_w$, and the
\textsf{adaptive}/\textsf{gated}/\textsf{offline} weights chosen.
Per (cell, seed, window, strategy) the run logs the resulting P@50.
